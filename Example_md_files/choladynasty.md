# The Chola Dynasty: A Maritime and Cultural Empire

The Chola Dynasty was one of the longest-ruling dynasties in world history. Based primarily in the fertile Kaveri River valley of Tamil Nadu, India, the Cholas established a powerful empire that dominated South India, Sri Lanka, and Southeast Asia between the 9th and 13th centuries CE.

---

## 1. Historical Timeline and Phases

The history of the Cholas is divided into three distinct phases: the Early Cholas of the Sangam literature, an interim period of decline, and the magnificent Imperial Cholas who established a global maritime footprint.

| Era | Timeline | Key Ruler / Characteristics | Historical Significance |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Early Cholas | 300 BCE – 300 CE | Karikala Chola | Sangam literature mentions, Kaveri delta rule |
| Interregnum | 300 CE – 850 CE | N/A | Obscurity under Kalabhras and Pallavas |
| Imperial Cholas | 850 CE – 1279 CE | Rajaraja I, Rajendra I | Golden age, maritime empire, massive temples |

## 3. Notable Rulers and Key Contributions

The success of the Imperial Cholas relied on a succession of exceptionally capable military strategists and administrative innovators.

| Ruler | Reign | Major Contribution |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Vijayalaya Chola** | 850–871 CE | Founded the Imperial Chola line; captured Thanjavur. |
| **Rajaraja Chola I** | 985–1014 CE | Built Brihadisvara Temple; conquered Sri Lanka & Maldives. |
| **Rajendra Chola I** | 1014–1044 CE | Expanded to Southeast Asia; built Gangaikonda Cholapuram. |
| **Kulottunga Chola I** | 1070–1122 CE | Abolished tolls; established strong trade ties with China. |

## 4. Architectural Excellence and Cultural Legacy

The Cholas were prolific builders and patrons of the arts. Their unique contribution to Dravidian architecture is preserved today in the "Great Living Chola Temples," a UNESCO World Heritage site.
